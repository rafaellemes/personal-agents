# Engineering Frameworks for High-Impact Narratives

These frameworks should be used to guide the questioning process and structure the final presentation.

## 1. STARL (Narrative Backbone)
- **S — Situation**: Objective context. What was the landscape?
- **T — Tarefa (Task)**: Specific responsibility. What was expected of YOU?
- **A — Action**: What you did, how, and why. (Use 4D here).
- **R — Result**: Measurable impact. (Use Impact Levels here).
- **L — Learning**: Evolution and scaling. How did this change the system?

## 2. 4D (Architecture & Technical Decisions)
Use these to dive deep into the **Action** phase:
- **1. Drivers**: What guided the decision? (Requirements, constraints, risks).
- **2. Design**: How was the solution structured?
- **3. Data**: Metrics, benchmarks, trade-offs. Why was this choice better than others?
- **4. Desdobramento (Deployment/Future)**: Impact on maintenance and evolution.

## 3. Impact Levels (Result Validation)
Every story should touch at least 2 of these:
- **Impacto Técnico**: Performance, architecture, reliability, cost.
- **Impacto no Time**: Mentorship, standards, unblocking, acceleration.
- **Impacto no Negócio**: Revenue, risk mitigation, roadmap acceleration.

## 4. Seniority Levels (The "System" View)
Demonstrate evolution of impact:
- **Nível 1 — Eu**: Individual execution.
- **Nível 2 — Nós**: Team enablement.
- **Nível 3 — Sistema**: Organizational, architectural, or cultural change.

## 5. Trade-offs (The "Why NOT" factor)
Crucial for Specialist roles:
- Alternatives considered.
- Why they were discarded.
- Risks accepted.
